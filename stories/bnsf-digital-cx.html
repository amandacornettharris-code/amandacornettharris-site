BNSF Railway

110+ Apps. One Customer Experience.

A failed $3M app became the case for a company-wide reframe — and a fully funded, five-year transformation of the digital experience at North America's largest railroad.

The Starting Point

A $3M app that couldn't hold ten users.

My first titled product job: a net-new track-and-trace app for intermodal, BNSF's largest business line. Testing told the truth the roadmap wouldn't — it crashed past ten concurrent users. I delivered the news that a project $3M in, over budget and overdue, would never ship.

The Diagnosis

It wasn't an intermodal problem.

The failed app did what dozens already did — differently. 110+ customer-facing apps, built in silos, giving different answers. Support spent 3× effort fixing customer errors. CSAT fell. The portal hadn't been touched since 2006 — before the iPhone.

Before · Duplicate Tools
Overlapping tools, one job each — the reason a customer could ask the same question and get different answers.
The Reframe

One problem, not one department's.

"We have a BNSF problem, not a department problem."
The pitch — to intermodal, technology, and marketing leadership
The strategy, in one image: scattered apps — each about BNSF — assembling into one experience built around the customer's job.
The Mandate

Five principles. Six departments. One CoP.

The pitch landed. I stood up a cross-department Customer Experience Community of Practice, governed by five principles:

  1. Do right by customers — upgrade the portal first.
  2. Work together, not against each other.
  3. Leverage what we own — build on Salesforce.
  4. Remove duplication; enhance where needed.
  5. Use data — and where there's none, talk to customers.
The Method

Where we had no data, we talked to customers.

Immersive Design Research
Mapping internal-vs-customer systems, then studying how the best consumer experiences are designed — applied to B2B rail.
The Outcome

The first portal update since before the iPhone.

A funded, five-year strategy across six departments — 14 net-new products, including the industry's first shipment Geo-Fence — plus apps retired and jobs modernized end to end. 80K+ users. ~$23B in annual business.

After · Live
One connected suite — Trace, Notifications, Railcar Management, Profile, Message Us, Geo-Fence — announced to customers by the Group VP, Consumer Products.
