Transforming
Experience Design

At North America's largest railroad, the Experience Design team was unfunded, mission-confused, and without organizational impact. Through a relentless focus on people, process, and measurable outcomes, Amanda transformed it into a 17-person powerhouse driving UX transformation across 40+ portfolios — and delivering results that reached from the boardroom to the field.

The Situation

Two teams. No shared mission. No organizational credibility.

When Amanda took over BNSF's Experience Design function in 2019, she inherited two separate teams — a UI/UX group and a Design Thinking group — that had been combined in 2017 but never truly unified. The organization offered 2 services, lacked a clear value proposition, and had limited standing with the business.

The team existed, but it didn't matter yet.

The Transformation

From 2 services to 7. From overlooked to indispensable.

Amanda rebuilt the team's operating model from the ground up — introducing pipeline management, relationship managers, quality checkpoints, CSAT, and NPS as the infrastructure of a real internal consultancy. She expanded the service portfolio from 2 to 7 distinct offerings, each designed to meet the business where it was.

Design Thinking
Tackle big, hard, unknown problems
UX & UI Design
GEL, wireframes, screen design, personas
User Research & Systems Thinking
Prototype testing, field observations, behavioral research
Project Readiness
Business process discovery, proposal blueprinting
Ideation & Innovation
XD Bootcamp, Trackathon, HCD training
Tech Evangelism
New tech scouting, virtual facilitation, remote support
Internal Services
TS communications, executive presentations

She also introduced 2 Relationship Managers — a concept borrowed from customer success — to build sustained, accountable partnerships with business units rather than one-off engagements.
