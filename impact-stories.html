5 Key Mindset Shifts for Building Agentforce Experiences

Co-authored by Amanda Harris and presented at Salesforce's TrailblazerDX (TDX), this session shares the five critical mindset shifts required to successfully deliver AI-powered Agentforce experiences — drawn from the real work of redesigning Salesforce Help to integrate Agentforce in just five days.

The Challenge

Salesforce Help receives 60 million visits per year, hosts 755,000 support articles across 85+ products in 17 languages, and generates 2 million support cases annually. Traditional support portals were making customers work too hard to find solutions. Leadership gave the team a clear directive: integrate Agentforce into Salesforce Help AND make self-service easier — in five days.

Mindset Shift #1: From Navigation to Conversation

When clicks become turns of a conversation, getting users started is key. The team shifted the entire interface and interaction model — replacing a traditional navigation-heavy portal with a conversation-first experience centered around the question: "How can Agentforce help?"

Mindset Shift #2: From Content Management to Knowledge Engineering

With 755K articles across 12+ authoring teams, the shift required rethinking information architecture entirely. Content management became knowledge engineering — leveraging Data Cloud's data model objects graph to make content not just findable, but intelligently retrievable by AI.

Mindset Shift #3: From Siloed Specialist to Cross-Functional Coalition

Two separate teams — MIAW (Messaging In-App & Web) and Customer Success — had to become one team overnight. Tooling was simplified to three essentials: Figma for design specs, Google Sheets for feature prioritization, and Slack as the central hub.

Mindset Shift #4: From User Interface to Flexible Engagement

The experience shifted from a static interface to flexible engagement where conversation context passes seamlessly to the messaging surface on any site page — maintaining continuity as customers move through the help experience.

Mindset Shift #5: From Building Features to Designing Outcomes

The team asked three questions: What do users need to be successful? What does data tell us about their patterns? What warrants keeping? By simplifying the experience, they made space for users to focus on core tasks: finding information and resolving issues. The result is live today at help.salesforce.com — with 397,000+ Agentforce conversations and 534,000+ human-handled requests.

Transforming Experience Design

At North America's largest railroad, the Experience Design team, initially unfunded and mission-confused, lacked organizational impact and clear objectives. Through a focus on people and structured processes, I transformed the team into a dynamic 17-person group, driving UX transformation across 40+ portfolios and leading over 320 design initiatives in 2020.

Origins

The Experience Design function at BNSF began as two separate services — Graphical UI & UX (2014) and Design Sprint Format (2015) — that were combined in 2017. Early work included CSS/GEL, RailPass, MAM, Workforce UX, Throughput Planner, and Customer Railcar Management. The team operated through Trackathons and XD Bootcamps but lacked a clear mission, structure, and organizational impact.

Building a 7-Service Operating Model

By 2020, I had transformed the team into a structured organization delivering seven distinct services: Design Thinking, User Experience & UI Design, User Research & Systems Thinking, Project Request Assessment & Readiness Enablement, Ideation & Innovation, Tech Evangelism & Remote Support, and Internal Services. The team was supported by pipeline management, two relationship managers, quality checkpoints, and CSAT & NPS accountability.

2020 Impact by the Numbers

The team delivered 320+ engagements across 12 departments and 8 divisions. This included 24 UX design projects, 146 icon designs, 43 tech evangelism engagements, 8 user research initiatives, and 7 project readiness assessments with 71 additional requests queued for 2021. The UI Community of Practice alone designed 13 reusable interactive components with a 400% ROI.

Technology Services Impact

Key contributions included Joint Facilities delivered on time and on budget, 6 customers using APIs in production with 12 in trial, SIDOL user research and data analysis, Gate System Modernization kiosk UX, and piloting the application review process for App Info Governance. The team also drove 68,669 TRACE/Message Us users and delivered tools including Industry Planner, Customer Local Service Notification, and Geofence.

Enterprise-Wide Impact

The team engaged 6 of 10 divisions plus the NOC, forged a ground-breaking partnership with System Safety, and drove HFREI reductions across multiple divisions — the NW Division was on pace for record lows and the RR Division achieved a 15.6% decrease. The team earned a 67 Net Promoter Score on operations engagements, exceeding the consulting industry average of 51. Work spanned HR, Law, Engineering, Transportation, and resulted in 3 patents through the Trackathon program.

2021 Strategic Focus

The forward roadmap centered on four priorities: Measured Design (integrating measurement into every service layer), Research as a Pillar (making user research a must-have for high-impact projects), Human-Centered Design Community (educating all of Technology Services on HCD methods), and Expanding Field Contact to reach every division and department by Q3.

Creating BNSF's Digital Customer Experience

I led a transformative initiative at BNSF to unify and modernize the digital customer experience, turning a fragmented tool ecosystem into a cohesive platform grounded in customer insights. By driving cross-functional alignment through design thinking, stakeholder engagement, and iterative validation, I secured a 5-year funded roadmap backed by executive sponsors.

My Role

This initiative spanned three levels of impact. As an Individual Contributor, I served as App PM leading scrum team development, originated the compelling vision, and owned organizational and executive communications to secure buy-in. As a Team Leader, I led PM and scrum team alignment, owned cross-team epic Design Thinking, and served as business leader and SME for app consolidation. As a Program Leader, I influenced at the enterprise level, developed the end-to-end customer experience strategy, created the customer research program, owned cross-business and program alignment, and managed VP+ sponsor communications.

The Starting Point: App Overload

BNSF had 94+ customer-facing applications, conceived and developed in silos, largely designed around BNSF's internal structure rather than customer needs. The experience was fragmented — conflicting interfaces, inconsistent navigation, and no unified view. Customers needed to do their jobs: find things easily and get things done. The vision was clear: consolidate the chaos into a cohesive experience designed from login forward.

The Approach

The plan combined four parallel workstreams: Customer Research to ground decisions in real user needs, Quick Improvements to build credibility and momentum, Rapid Prototyping & Validation to test ideas before investing in development, and Strong Share Partnerships to align stakeholders across the organization.

Radical Alignment

Design Thinking and Design Sprints brought together SMEs, business owners, operations leaders, IT, Product, Engineering, UX, and customer owners — because aligned thinking aligns priorities. Planning together went beyond talking together. And communication was relentless: full program meetings every three weeks ensured everyone heard the same message the same way, which proved less expensive and time-consuming than scattered one-off meetings.

The Outcome

The work resulted in a 5-year funded plan to transform BNSF's digital customer experience. New tools launched included Trace, Industry Notifications, Railcar Management, APIs, Profile, Message Us, and Geo-Fence — all within a unified, modern platform. In 2023, the Group Vice President of Consumer Products communicated the new customer tools directly to BNSF's customer base, and the initiative was featured in Rail Talk.

What I Learned

Be Bold: put customers first in action, in vision, and when seeking sponsorship. Communicate More: talking together doesn't equal alignment — communicate, repeat, and invite conversation. Be Adaptable: user success is customer success, test ideas before developing, customers want to be asked, no idea is precious, plans will change, and seeking dissent drives better outcomes.
