5 Key Mindset Shifts for Building Agentforce Experiences

Co-authored and presented at TDX. Redesigned Salesforce Help to integrate Agentforce in 5 days, achieving 82% self-service resolution and 2.2M+ AI-powered interactions.

The Challenge

Salesforce Help receives 60 million visits per year, hosts 755,000 support articles across 85+ products in 17 languages, and generates 2 million support cases annually. Leadership gave the team a clear directive: integrate Agentforce into Salesforce Help AND make self-service easier — in five days.

Mindset Shift #1: From Navigation to Conversation

When clicks become turns of a conversation, getting users started is key. The team shifted the entire interface and interaction model — replacing a traditional navigation-heavy portal with a conversation-first experience.

Mindset Shift #2: From Content Management to Knowledge Engineering

With 755K articles across 12+ authoring teams, content management became knowledge engineering — leveraging Data Cloud's data model objects graph to make content intelligently retrievable by AI.

Mindset Shift #3: From Siloed Specialist to Cross-Functional Coalition

Two separate teams — MIAW and Customer Success — had to become one team overnight. Tooling was simplified to three essentials: Figma, Google Sheets, and Slack.

Mindset Shift #4: From User Interface to Flexible Engagement

Conversation context passes seamlessly to the messaging surface on any site page — maintaining continuity as customers move through the help experience.

Mindset Shift #5: From Building Features to Designing Outcomes

By simplifying the experience, they made space for users to focus on core tasks: finding information and resolving issues. The result is live at help.salesforce.com — with 397,000+ Agentforce conversations and 534,000+ human-handled requests.

Origins

The Experience Design function began as two separate services combined in 2017. The team lacked a clear mission, structure, and organizational impact.

Building a 7-Service Operating Model

By 2020, I transformed the team into a structured organization delivering seven distinct services with pipeline management, relationship managers, quality checkpoints, and NPS accountability.

2020 Impact by the Numbers

320+ engagements across 12 departments and 8 divisions. 24 UX design projects, 146 icon designs, 43 tech evangelism engagements. The UI CoP designed 13 reusable components with a 400% ROI.

Enterprise-Wide Impact

Engaged 6 of 10 divisions plus the NOC. Drove HFREI reductions — NW Division on pace for record lows, RR Division achieved 15.6% decrease. Earned 67 NPS exceeding industry average of 51. 3 patents through Trackathon.

2021 Strategic Focus

Four priorities: Measured Design, Research as a Pillar, Human-Centered Design Community, and Expanding Field Contact to every division and department by Q3.

Creating BNSF's Digital Customer Experience

Unified 94+ fragmented customer applications into a cohesive digital platform. Secured a 5-year funded transformation roadmap launching Trace, Geofence, and Railcar Management.

My Role

Spanned three levels: IC originating the vision and owning exec comms, Team Leader driving cross-team Design Thinking, and Program Leader developing E2E customer experience strategy with VP+ sponsor management.

The Starting Point: App Overload

94+ customer-facing apps developed in silos. Fragmented interfaces, inconsistent navigation, no unified view. The vision: consolidate chaos into a cohesive experience designed from login forward.

Radical Alignment

Design Sprints brought together SMEs, business owners, operations, IT, Product, Engineering, UX, and customers. Full program meetings every three weeks — everyone hears the same message the same way.

The Outcome

5-year funded plan to transform digital CX. Launched Trace, Industry Notifications, Railcar Management, APIs, Profile, Message Us, and Geo-Fence within a unified modern platform.

What I Learned

Be Bold: customers first in action and vision. Communicate More: talking doesn't equal alignment. Be Adaptable: no idea is precious, plans will change, seeking dissent drives better outcomes.
